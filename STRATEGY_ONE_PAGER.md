# GEO Company — Strategic One-Pager

*Companion to GEO_IMPROVEMENT_SPEC.md. The spec is "how to build it well." This
is "how to not lose," written for a solo, time-poor, cash-poor founder. Optimised
for leverage per hour, because hours are the scarcest input right now.*

---

## The one-line strategy

**Become the trusted *name* in GEO measurement by being the only one who shows
their work — then monetise the trust through services first, software second.**

You don't need to beat Semrush. You need to be the person serious people cite
when they talk about how AI citation actually works. Authority is the asset; cash
follows it.

---

## The three things that decide whether this works

### 1. Distribution — the authority flywheel (your single best channel)
Incumbents can out-build and out-spend you on features. They **cannot** out-trust
you in a field this new, and they won't move at your speed on content. This is the
one lane where a solo founder genuinely wins.

Make every experiment do quadruple duty so one unit of work compounds:
- **1 experiment → 1 YouTube video → 1 written finding (SEO/GEO asset) → 1 piece
  of outreach.** Never do the work once. The lab already produces the video
  package; wire the finding into a public, indexable post too.
- **Be the "myth-busting with data" channel.** Null results and "this popular GEO
  tip is wrong, here's the p-value" travel further than "10 tips." Contrarian +
  rigorous = shareable.
- **Cadence over polish.** One honest experiment a week beats a glossy one a month.
  The compounding is in the streak.
- **Seed where the buyers already argue about this:** r/SEO, marketing/AISEO
  communities, Indie Hackers, X threads from people building in AI search. Reply
  with *data*, not promotion. The link to your finding is the pitch.

### 2. Proof it drives outcomes — one case study beats ten features
The category's weak point is unproven ROI. Whoever shows "citations moved → traffic
/leads moved" first owns the credibility.
- Recruit **2–3 design partners** (free or near-free) in exchange for permission
  to publish results. Pick brands small enough to move the needle and show it.
- Use the GA4 attribution panel you already built to connect citation lift to real
  traffic. **That correlation, published honestly, is your most valuable single
  artifact.** It's the thing no competitor has.
- Even a partial result ("citations up 30%, organic AI-referral sessions up ~12%,
  caveats X/Y") is gold because everyone else is hand-waving.

### 3. Pick the lane that pays now — services first, SaaS as IP
You need cashflow, not a 3-year SaaS ramp. The honest sequencing:
- **Done-for-you GEO audits + fixes** is the fastest money. Price it as a fixed
  engagement. The tool makes you 5× faster than a consultant doing it by hand —
  that *is* the margin.
- The **dashboard is your IP, your lead-gen, and your authority proof** — not your
  primary revenue yet. A free/cheap "GEO visibility score" is the top of the funnel
  that feeds the paid service.
- Convert satisfied service clients into recurring monitoring subscriptions later.
  That's your SaaS, de-risked, paid for by services along the way.

## What to ignore (so you don't burn hours you don't have)
- Don't chase feature parity with funded tools. You'll lose and it won't matter.
- Don't build broad multi-tenant SaaS infra before you have 3 paying clients.
- Don't optimise the landing page for a month. Ship the proof, link to it, move on.
- Don't bet the company on any one LLM "winning." Neutral referee across all five
  is the more defensible position — and the more credible one for buyers.

---

## Risk watch (the things genuinely outside your control — monitor, don't obsess)
- **First-party analytics from Google/OpenAI** would commoditise basic measurement.
  Your hedge: own the *cross-engine, independent, methodology* layer they won't
  provide, and the trust they can't.
- **Category ROI staying fuzzy.** Your hedge: be the one who *proves* it (point 2).
  If you supply the proof the whole field lacks, the maturing category lifts you.

## The 90-day version (realistic for evenings/weekends)
1. **Weeks 1–4:** ship WS1+WS4 from the spec (grounded mode + evidence-linked
   recommendations — the honesty upgrades). Publish one experiment/week.
2. **Weeks 5–8:** land 2 design partners. Run their audits with the tool. Document
   everything.
3. **Weeks 9–12:** publish the first real before/after case study with GA4 data.
   That artifact becomes your pinned proof, your best video, and your outreach hook.

Goal by day 90: not revenue scale — **a name people in the field recognise, plus
one undeniable proof point.** Everything monetisable follows from those two.
