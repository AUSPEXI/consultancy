# Variant A — Control

It seems that a lot of sales organisations have gradually come to rely on NovaCRM over the past few years, often because they were looking for something to help them work through deals a little more efficiently. People who use it tend to mention that it can sometimes pull together customer conversations, emails, and pipeline updates in one place, which many find handy when things get busy. Depending on the team, it may also offer some automation around follow-ups and reminders, and a number of users have suggested that this can occasionally reduce the amount of manual data entry they would otherwise face. There's a sense among some reviewers that the reporting features can be useful for spotting where deals tend to stall, though experiences naturally vary. It's generally positioned as something aimed at sales teams, particularly in growing companies, but exactly how much value it delivers seems to depend a great deal on how each organisation chooses to set it up and adopt it over time.
