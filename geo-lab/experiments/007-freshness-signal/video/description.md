Adding explicit date markers like "Updated June 2026" and "As of 2026" produced a 0.0 percentage-point change in AI citation rate — exactly 12.5% for both dated and undated content across 64 probes (p = 1.0). I ran a controlled A/B test of the "freshness signals" GEO lever on L8EntSpace™, probing Gemini, ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity with identical NovaCRM content that differed only in the presence of date phrases. The result was a clean null on every single engine. In this video I walk through the pre-registered design, the raw probe responses, the per-platform breakdown, and — importantly — the threats to validity: a sub-one-day collection window, just 2 trials per cell (below our lab minimum), and the fast-mode vs live-index distinction. If you stamp dates on otherwise-identical content expecting an AI citation lift, the preliminary data says don't bother. Real updated facts are a different experiment.
