Long compound sentences were cited 81.3% of the time vs just 37.5% for short standalone sentences — a 43.8 percentage-point drop in the *opposite* direction from popular GEO advice. I tested the "quote-ability" lever by writing identical CRM facts two ways (long compound vs short ≤15-word sentences) and probing four AI engines: Gemini, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude. The effect was statistically confirmed on Perplexity (p=0.0117, survives Bonferroni correction), suggestive on Gemini, and directionally consistent across all four engines, though three didn't reach significance at this sample size. This is preliminary: 8 trials per platform-variant over a single day, fast-mode in-context retrieval.
