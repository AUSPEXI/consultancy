Moving a single citable sentence from paragraph 3 to sentence 1 raised AI citation rate from 6.3% to 43.8% — a +37.5pp lift (p=0.0005) across 64 probes on four engines. In this GEO experiment I test the "inverted pyramid" lever: does front-loading your key claim actually get you cited more by AI? I ran identical content — same statistic, same supporting facts, same word count — with the only difference being *where* the claim sits. Results split hard by engine: Gemini and Claude showed large, significant gains, Perplexity leaned positive but wasn't conclusive, and GPT-4o-mini cited neither variant. I also walk through the honest limitations: n=2 per cell, a single-day collection window, fast-mode retrieval (not live index), and multiple-comparison correction.
